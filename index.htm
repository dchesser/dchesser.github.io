A wireframe model of a sub-urban home, rendered from the foundation.
Freshly-doped silicon motherboards on a saw-horse, cut down to kilobyte-sized pieces.
Neighborhood ne'er-do-wells hover-surf down the sidewalk on their distended keyboards, customized in Dvorak just to get a rise.

A moving van finally arrived after taking exit 200 off of the information superhighway.
In the driver's seat, a fresh Web-master looks on in pride at his future endeavor.
At last, a home for him and his thinking engines adorned in cheap black plastic.

But first, dibs must be claimed, for the early bird catches the worm.
Then the address must be given a name, which the Web-master decrees "dchesser dot website".
Then the address must be given an actual place, probably somewhere on the Web-master's GitHub account.

There will be plenty of time to unpack the gratuitous palm trees and pastel neon lamps once this home is rendered.
